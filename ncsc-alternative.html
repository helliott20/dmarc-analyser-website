Frequently Asked Questions

Is NCSC Mail Check really being retired?

Yes. The NCSC announced both services will be retired by 31 March 2026. But importantly, aggregate DMARC reporting already stopped in March 2025 - so if you relied on Mail Check for DMARC visibility, that's already gone.

Can I access my Mail Check data with DMARC Analyser?

Historical Mail Check data cannot be imported directly. However, DMARC Analyser begins collecting full reports immediately upon setup. You'll have comprehensive reporting going forward.

How long does migration take?

Basic setup takes under 5 minutes. Either connect your Gmail account via OAuth or update DMARC record email addresses. Reports arrive within 24 hours.

Is DMARC Analyser suitable for large institutions with 100+ domains?

Yes. Self-hosting works well for large institutions - deploy it on your own servers and manage as many domains as you need. There are no licensing restrictions.

What if I need help with the migration?

Yes. DMARC Analyser is GDPR-compliant. We provide transparent data handling, clear privacy policies, and the option to self-host for full data control.
